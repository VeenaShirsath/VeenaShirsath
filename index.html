I am a second year student in the MS in Economics and Computation program of Duke University. I hold a bachelors degree in Economics from Ahmedabad University. I am passionate about climate and energy, and their intersection with development, urbanization and wellbeing. My general interests lie in climate-related risk, energy transition, circular economy, and adaptation and mitigation finance/policy. I have worked on projects analyzing utility data for effective demand-side management, residential cooling demand  and, load characteristics. I am interested in applying quantitative tools and interdisciplinary collaboration to advance sustainable and equitable energy solutions. Before finding my interest in climate change and energy, I explored urbanization, socio-economic development, and urban migration through several research projects and internships.
